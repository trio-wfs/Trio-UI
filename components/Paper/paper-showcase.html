Paper

Static surface primitive. Five layered tiers, border-stroke only — no shadows at rest. Hover-shadow behavior lives on interactive wrappers, not on Paper itself.

layers Overview

Paper is the foundational surface primitive in TRIO. Use it for any boxed content — cards, panels, containers, sidebars. Five level values cover the full surface hierarchy from outermost page canvas to emphasized accent.

No shadows at rest. If a surface needs hover-shadow feedback (e.g. clickable cards in a grid), the interactive parent component owns the shadow — Paper stays static.

filter_none Subtle in context — composing with the parent

The subtle tier is a 10% black overlay. It composes with whatever sits behind it — rendering darker on lighter parents, less prominent on darker ones. Use sparingly: introduce only when a nested-container delineation is genuinely needed.

Parent: paper (#FFFFFF)
Nested subtle — renders as ~#FAFAFA
Parent: secondary (#FAFAFA)
Nested subtle — renders as ~#F4F4F4 (1 step down)
Parent: accent (#E4F7FD)
Nested subtle — renders as a slightly darker tint of the accent

lightbulb Best Practices

  • Default to level="paper". White is the workhorse for cards, panels, and containers.
  • Use level="secondary" for content wrappers below headers. Groups all the page's components without competing with cards inside.
  • Use level="subtle" for nested containers where you want a 1-step-down feel without committing to a fixed gray. Best inside paper or accent parents.
  • Use level="accent" sparingly — for highlighted or pinned content (e.g. canonical answer blocks in Q&A threads).
  • Don't add shadows. If a surface needs hover feedback, wrap Paper in an interactive component that owns the shadow.
  • For semantic surfaces (warning, error, success), use <Alert>, not Paper. Paper handles neutral surface hierarchy; Alert handles semantic feedback.
